Lago di Braies

Lago di Braies, also known as Pragser Wildsee, is a stunning alpine lake located in the Prags Dolomites in South Tyrol, northern Italy. It is situated within the Fanes-Sennes-Prags Nature Park, surrounded by the picturesque Dolomite mountains. Here are some key features and information about Lago di Braies: Location: Lago di Braies is situated in the Braies Valley, about 97 kilometers north of Bolzano, the capital of South Tyrol. Scenery: The lake is renowned for its crystal-clear, emerald-green waters and the breathtaking backdrop of the Dolomites. The reflection of the mountains on the lake's surface creates a picturesque and serene atmosphere. Activities: Visitors to Lago di Braies can enjoy a variety of activities. The lake itself offers opportunities for boating and rowing, and there are walking and hiking trails around the lake, providing stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Boat Rentals: Traditional wooden rowboats are available for rent at the lake, allowing visitors to paddle on the tranquil waters and take in the scenic beauty. Historical Significance: The lake is a popular destination and has historical significance. It was used as a location for the Italian TV series "Un passo dal cielo" and gained international fame after being featured in the movie "The Italian Job." Hiking Trails: The area around Lago di Braies offers several hiking trails of varying difficulty levels. One popular trail is the circular path around the lake, providing stunning views and photo opportunities. Seasonal Variations: Lago di Braies is a year-round destination. In winter, the surrounding area becomes a winter wonderland with opportunities for snowshoeing and cross-country skiing. Tourist Facilities: The lake has tourist facilities, including a parking area, a hotel (Hotel Pragser Wildsee), and a restaurant. During peak tourist seasons, the area can get crowded, so it's advisable to plan your visit accordingly. Lago di Braies is not only a natural gem but also a cultural and recreational hub, attracting visitors from around the world who seek the beauty of the Dolomites and the tranquility of this alpine lake.
